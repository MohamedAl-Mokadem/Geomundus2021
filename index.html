Overview

GeoMundus is a free international Conference organized by students for students. The organizing committee is the students from the Erasmus Mundus Master of Science in Geospatial Technologies. The conference aims to share cutting-edge scientific research, knowledge, and skills in the fields of Geospatial Technologies, Geoinformatics, and Geosciences, including but not limited to geographic information systems and sciences,
spatial cognition, geography, and spatial data sciences. It is an opportunity to learn about and share scientific research, knowledge, and skills with other students and researchers of all things ‘Geo’. Every year it will give participants the chance to learn about technological advancements of GI, research activities and applications of geospatial technologies in today’s world. GeoMundus is unique, featuring workshops where participants can experience first-hand the future of the field as well as a career session specially designed for students interested in a geospatial career.

Geospatial Technologies: Fancy toys, or tangible outcomes?

Recent decades have seen an explosion of creativity in geospatial technologies, applications and methodologies. As members of the geospatial communities we think it is important to stop and question the utility of these new innovations. In this year's conference we seek to engage with popular and academic debates about whether innovations in geospatial technologies are driven by technologies or application. We want to challenge the participants to think critically about these questions and to ask themselves what the value of their and their pariworks are to society.
Please see Program Section for more detailed information.

Who should participate in GeoMundus 2021?

Everyone who is curious about geospatial technologies and their applications. We especially welcome students, researchers, industry leaders, and interested members of the public. It is not only a great opportunity to learn, but also to network with the brightest minds in this exciting field.

GeoMundus History

The first GeoMundus conference was held successfully in 2009 at ISEGI, Lisbon, Portugal. Since then, the conference has been held annually always alternating between Castellón (Spain), Münster (Germany) and Lisbon (Portugal) as central locations.
